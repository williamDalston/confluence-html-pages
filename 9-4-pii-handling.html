PII Safe Handling Guidance

This page provides guidance for safely handling Personally Identifiable Information (PII) in analytics work. Proper PII handling ensures compliance with privacy regulations and protects sensitive information.

What is PII?

PII includes any information that can be used to identify an individual, such as:

  • Names and contact information
  • Social Security numbers
  • Email addresses and usernames
  • IP addresses and device identifiers
  • Location data

PII Handling Principles

  • Minimize: Collect only necessary PII
  • Secure: Encrypt PII in transit and at rest
  • Limit Access: Restrict access to authorized personnel only
  • Retain Minimally: Retain PII only as long as necessary
  • Dispose Securely: Properly dispose of PII when no longer needed

Analytics Considerations

  • Aggregate data to remove PII where possible
  • Use anonymization techniques when needed
  • Avoid including PII in dashboards and reports
  • Implement data masking in development environments

Compliance

Ensure PII handling complies with:

  • Privacy Act of 1974
  • HIPAA regulations (if applicable)
  • HHS privacy policies
  • Federal cybersecurity requirements

Incident Response

If PII is exposed or compromised:

  1. Immediately report to security team
  2. Follow incident response procedures
  3. Document the incident
  4. Implement corrective actions

Resources

For questions or concerns about PII handling:

  • Contact HHS Privacy Office
  • Reference HHS privacy policies
  • Consult with security and compliance teams
