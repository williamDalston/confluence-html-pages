Content Reading Level (Flesch-Kincaid)

This page documents content reading level analysis using the Flesch-Kincaid readability test. Reading level metrics help ensure content is accessible to target audiences.

What is Flesch-Kincaid?

The Flesch-Kincaid Reading Ease test measures how easy text is to read. Scores range from 0-100, with higher scores indicating easier-to-read content.

Reading Level Categories

  • 90-100: Very Easy (5th grade level)
  • 80-89: Easy (6th grade level)
  • 70-79: Fairly Easy (7th grade level)
  • 60-69: Standard (8th-9th grade level)
  • 50-59: Fairly Difficult (10th-12th grade level)
  • 30-49: Difficult (College level)
  • 0-29: Very Difficult (College graduate level)

HHS Content Goals

Target reading levels for HHS content:

  • General Public Content: 60-70 (Standard to Fairly Easy)
  • Technical Content: 50-60 (Fairly Difficult to Standard)
  • Internal Documentation: 50-70 (Standard to Fairly Difficult)

Analysis & Reporting

Reading level metrics are:

  • Calculated for all content pages
  • Tracked in Power BI content dashboards
  • Used to identify content needing simplification
  • Monitored for compliance with accessibility standards

Improvement Strategies

  • Use shorter sentences and simpler words
  • Break up long paragraphs
  • Avoid jargon and technical terms when possible
  • Use headings and lists to improve structure
