Purpose of This Power BI Workspace (HHS Analytics)

This Confluence page serves as the central knowledge hub for all Power BI activities supporting the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS). Its purpose is to create a unified, well-documented environment where the analytics team can collaborate, share standards, and accelerate development.

What This Page Provides

  • Documentation of all Power BI datasets, dashboards, and models used across HHS.
  • Technical guidance on data connections, refresh strategies, API usage, and performance optimization.
  • Version-controlled templates for reports, DAX patterns, semantic models, and layout standards.
  • Best practices for governance, security, accessibility, and data integrity.
  • Onboarding support for new analysts, including tool access, workflow setup, and development guidelines.
  • Cross-team alignment between Engineering, UXD, Content, and Analytics, making sure reporting follows approved architecture.

Who This Page Is For

This space is intended for:

  • HHS BI Developers and Data Analysts
  • Power BI Report Authors and Stakeholders
  • Engineers and DevOps working with data pipelines
  • Product Owners and content teams requiring insights

Why This Matters

Power BI plays a key role in enabling data-driven decision-making across HHS. This page ensures our analytics work is:

  • Consistent – aligned with approved definitions and architecture
  • Transparent – every dataset and report is properly documented
  • Secure – following HHS and federal compliance requirements
  • Efficient – reducing manual work through reusable patterns

Together, this Confluence space anchors a collaborative, scalable, and compliant BI ecosystem for the entire HHS analytics function.
