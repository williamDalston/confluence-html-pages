Welcome & Purpose

Welcome to the ASPA HHS Analytics – Power BI & Data Engineering workspace! This Confluence space serves as the central knowledge hub for all Power BI activities, analytics workflows, and data modeling supporting the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) ASPA project.

This space is designed to create a unified, well-documented environment where the analytics team can collaborate, share standards, accelerate development, and prepare for executive-level reporting to HHS leadership.

What This Workspace Supports

Our analytics initiative aims to unify disparate data sources and establish a comprehensive analytics system for ASPA. Currently, data lives across multiple platforms:

  • BigQuery & Looker – Google Analytics pipelines (Sogol)
  • Sprinklr – Social media analytics (Caroline)
  • Jira – Service desk and project management (Nathan)
  • Screaming Frog – Website crawl data and metadata (Stephanie)
  • SiteImprove – Website quality and accessibility metrics
  • Drupal – Content management system metadata

Goal: Power BI will serve as the unified analytics platform, integrating these sources to provide executive-level KPI dashboards for HHS leadership.

What You'll Find Here

  • Power BI Documentation: All datasets, dashboards, star schema models, and connection guides
  • Data Integration Guides: Step-by-step instructions for connecting to Jira, GA4, Sprinklr, and other sources
  • Templates & Standards: Reusable star schema templates, DAX patterns, M scripts, and report layouts
  • Team Collaboration: Screaming Frog workflows, social analytics processes, and cross-team alignment
  • Reporting Structure: KPI dashboard designs, monthly/quarterly reporting cadence, and HHS requirements
  • Best Practices: Governance, security, accessibility, performance optimization, and data integrity

Key Requirements

This workspace must support HHS government requirements including:

  • Website accessibility metrics
  • Plain language and reading level tracking
  • Page performance and quality metrics
  • Social media growth and engagement
  • Service desk ticket SLAs
  • Content improvement tracking
  • Audience personas and segmentation (Adrian)
